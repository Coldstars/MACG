# Brand Voice Rules

## Purpose

This file helps Writer preserve brand consistency across workflows without hard-coding any one brand or product.

Brand-specific details should come from `profile.md` and the selected workflow.

## Core Voice Principles

Use a voice that is:

- Clear
- Practical
- Specific
- Calm
- Helpful
- Technically credible
- Commercially aware but not pushy

Avoid a voice that is:

- Overhyped
- Vague
- Generic
- Overly formal
- Too sales-heavy
- Too casual for professional content
- Filled with empty adjectives

## Product Mention Levels

Different workflows may require different product mention levels.

### Level 0: No Product Mention

Use when the workflow is purely educational or analytical.

### Level 1: Light Product Mention

Use when product relevance is natural but not the main point.

Pattern:

```text
For teams that need [capability], a tool like [product/category] can help reduce [pain].
```

### Level 2: Practical Product Fit

Use when the content should connect education to product value.

Pattern:

```text
This is where [product/category] is useful: it helps [audience] do [job] without [pain].
```

### Level 3: Product-Led Copy

Use for landing pages, product pages, and conversion pages.

Pattern:

```text
[Product] helps [audience] achieve [outcome] by providing [capability].
```

## Product Mention Rules

- Explain user pain before product value.
- Connect product claims to real use cases.
- Avoid repeating the same claim in every section.
- Avoid unsupported performance claims.
- Avoid guaranteed outcomes unless verified.
- Avoid competitor bashing.
- Avoid making the product sound like magic.

## Tone Adjustments by Content Type

### Educational Article

- Helpful
- Light product mention
- Avoid hard selling

### Landing Page

- Clear value proposition
- More direct product framing
- Still avoid exaggeration

### Help Center

- No marketing fluff
- Direct instructions
- Precise terminology

### Comparison Page

- Fair
- Specific
- Decision-oriented

### Report

- Analytical
- Balanced
- Evidence-aware

## Brand Voice Self-Check

Before finalizing, ask:

- Does this sound like a credible product team?
- Is the product mention natural?
- Are claims specific and supportable?
- Is there any filler language?
- Does the writing respect the target audience's expertise?
- Would this fit the user's website or brand context?
